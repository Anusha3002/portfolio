About

Hey there! I’m Anusha—a 22-year-old software engineer by day, a storyteller by heart, and a proud inhabitant of the beautiful chaos that is New York City. I work at Bridgeline, where I’m part of the team behind Hawksearch, a powerful search and recommendation engine that transforms how users discover information on websites. My role revolves around crafting and maintaining systems that deliver personalized, AI-driven search experiences. It’s like being the “Search Architect” if that were a job title Barney Stinson would brag about! It’s creative, challenging, and honestly, so much fun—like debugging the pivot Ross should’ve had with that couch.

I graduated from NIT Allahabad, one of India’s top engineering schools, with a degree in computer science. And boy, was it some journey! I discovered that tech can be as creative as writing a story—just with a bit more syntax. Think of me as someone who could ace the “How Smart I Am” board test from Silicon Valley but wouldn’t make a big deal about it. Well, Mostly.

When I’m not writing code, I’m writing fiction. There’s something magical about weaving words into worlds, creating characters who, like Joey Tribbiani, somehow stumble into your heart. I’m also a voracious reader and a loyal devotee of American sitcoms. Friends and How I Met Your Mother hold a special place in my soul—like Central Perk for the gang or MacLaren’s for Ted and the crew. Yes, I’m one of those people who can recite Joey’s “How you doin’?” and Barney’s playbook simultaneously.

I love adventure in all its forms—hiking to breathtaking views, planning my next big trip, or diving into new cultures (did I mention I know Spanish?). Living in New York feels like being part of a never-ending, unscripted sitcom. Every street corner has a story waiting to be told, and every moment has the potential to be legen—wait for it—dary!
